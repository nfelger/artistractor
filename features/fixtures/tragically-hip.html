“Locked in the Trunk of a Car” by The Tragically Hip
(Words/Music: The Tragically Hip, Album: Glastonbury and Elsewhere Bootleg ca. 1991)
  
Otter-heart posted a simple statement that seems to be part of my theme today. She said that she loves the improvisation of Gord Downie. From there I began to revel in how Gord’s ramblings and improvisations are an essential part of the creative process of the band. This led me to dig up some old Hip bootlegs (always an enjoyable way to kick off a long weekend) and I re-stumbled upon this gem of a performance of “Locked in the Trunk of a Car” in which gord introduces the song by saying:
 
This is a song for Salman Rushdie.
He plays left wing for the Leafs.
That was a falsehood.

And just as the “let me out” section begins, he claims that a “little tiny cat” is the one crying “let me out.”

Finally, at the end of the song, he quotes a famous passage by 17th century philosopher Blaise Pascal:

All man’s misery stems from a single cause
My inability to remain quietly
Sit still
Yeah, Pascal, he’d tell y’all to sit still now

Yeah, so in one song, Gord ad-libs about hockey, a famous author, kittens, and philosophy. Kudos, Gord.
